What is Jaldoot?

'Water for All' Jaldoot is a project undertaken by Atlas Copco in co-operation with the NGO Sevavardhini

Who is a Jaldoot?

The Hindi word Jaldoot literally means Water Messenger

Why Jaldoot?

This project aims to work within a number of villages in the semi-arid drought-prone areas of Maharashtra, India, which has seen a number of tragic farmer suicides in the past few years, due to recurrent low rainfall. This erratic rainfall has also resulted in depletion of ground water, which is the major source of water, especially for drinking. Over 27 villages have been identified, with a total population of around 50,000 people. Provision of drinking water is the main deliverable. The aim also is to initiate and foster community-developed water resource development and conservation plans, that will also help reduce over-dependency on the monsoon for agriculture, which is the regionsâ€™ main source of employment and income. In other words, involvement and training of local people in water management systems is envisaged through Jaldoots.
